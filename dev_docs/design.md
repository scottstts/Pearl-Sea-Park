# Sea Park — Game Design (Canon)

> **Status: CONFIRMED** by Scott on 2026-07-09 (amendments integrated below). Implementation plan: `dev_docs/plan.md`.

## The fantasy in one line

For one golden day, you are the only guest in the most beautiful place humans ever built — and they built it at the bottom of the sea.

## What kind of game this is

A first-person "walking wonder" experience. The game *is* the place. Design references are theme-park craft (sightlines, landmark "weenies", pacing between reveals) more than other video games. BioShock's Rapture is the deliberate opposite: this park is sunlit, immaculate, benevolent. Nothing is wrong here.

## Art direction (the confirmed north star)

**Photorealism combined with a dream-like feel.** Physically grounded rendering, real scale, real light transport — with deliberate dream levers on top: impossible water clarity, a pushed turquoise/gold palette, soft glowing bloom, perpetual gentle motion, and slightly plump, toy-like silhouettes on ride vehicles and props (the "little cartoonish" note lives in shape language, never in shading — no toon rendering). The player should be pleasantly surprised and amazed; wonder is the metric.

The built world is Belle Époque / Art Nouveau (~1905): glass-and-iron domes, whiplash curves, brass, verdigris, mother-of-pearl, white marble, mosaic. Music-box waltzes.

## The dream logic (core world model)

- **The sea behaves like air.** You walk normally on the seabed; rides run like air rides; nothing floats, nobody swims. Underwater is purely how the world *looks and sounds* — fog-blue distance, glints raking down, rays and jellies overhead. This is never explained. Dream logic, stated nowhere, felt everywhere.
- **The eternal golden afternoon.** One fixed time of day: sun angled so light shafts and caustic glints pour through the surface across the park. No day/night cycle. The Grotto dark ride supplies the game's darkness contrast.
- **Nobody home but you.** No humans, no automatons, no characters of any kind. The park runs itself — machinery is the staff, ticket machines punch your ticket, the rides cycle on their timetable. The park's guests are the sea creatures.

## Premise & fiction (ambient only)

A visionary founder built "The Pearl — Royal Pleasure Gardens Beneath the Sea" on a sunlit reef shelf. Today is a private preview day: you hold Golden Ticket No. 1. Story is delivered only by the environment — plaques, postcards, a one-room founder's museum. No dialogue, no quests, no text walls. The quiet theme: *the sea came to visit us.*

## The setting

A turquoise shelf at ~26 m depth. Above everything hangs the **Silver Ceiling** — the underside of the surface, always visible, always moving, sun wobbling in it, Snell's window overhead. North rim: a sheer **drop-off** into deep blue void. South/west: kelp forest curtain. Between the architecture: white sand, coral gardens, seagrass meadows. Park scale ~600 m across, hub-and-spoke, a landmark always on the horizon. Motif: the park plays on **both sides of the waterline** — two attractions deliberately breach the surface.

## Places & attractions

1. **The Descent Bell (arrival, re-ridable).** Start above water on a buoy pavilion — the Wheel's crest turning out of the waves in the distance. A glass diving bell descends through the waterline in one unbroken shot: sky → foam → blue → the park blazing below.
2. **Grand Atrium.** Cathedral entrance dome; a brass ticket machine punches your golden ticket; a brass scale model of the park (you take the pocket version — the only map).
3. **The Esplanade.** Barrel-vaulted glass boulevard, arcade shopfronts, banners swaying, and the scheduled manta crossing above the vault.
4. **Tidal Court (hub).** Open lagoon plaza ringed by a colonnade; the **Bubble Fountain** at center — the Bellagio inverted, dancing with choreographed air and light. Grand show on the schedule.
5. **The Great Wheel (east).** ~40 m wheel, twelve nautilus-shell glass gondolas. Its crest **breaks the surface** — each revolution gives you seconds of open sky and sun, water sheeting off the glass, then blue again. The park's castle.
6. **The Torrent (north, on the drop-off).** Glass-tube coaster, brass torpedo train: launch → dive **off the shelf edge into open void** → thread a shipwreck → breach the surface hump → home. Real coaster physics, ~90 s.
7. **Menagerie Gardens (west).** The inverted zoo: the **Sun Garden** (a greenhouse of flowers and butterflies); the **Jellyfish Court** (spiraling cloister through drifting moon jellies); the **Turtle Lagoon** (feed them); the **Leviathan Overlook** on the rim — where the whale passes on schedule, shadow first, then the eye.
8. **The Midway (south).** Boardwalk hall, calliope rag, warm bulbs: the **Carrousel des Abysses** (two decks of seahorse/dolphin/turtle/ray/narwhal mounts + nautilus chariot); **Ring the Narwhal**, **Pearl Diver** skee-ball, **Kraken Bell** hammer — real physics toys with silly prizes (wearable paper hat); a sweets kiosk where you can hold an ice cream that slowly melts and does nothing else. Perfect.
9. **Grotto of Pearls (southeast).** Dark boat ride through caverns: bioluminescent gardens, a shell-organ kinetic sculpture, a pearl treasury glowing like a galaxy, one gentle drop. ~4 min. The park's night.
10. **The Pearl Line.** Brass cable-gondola loop 14 m above the park, two stations — transit that is secretly the aerial tour.
11. **Texture rooms.** Café Méduse terrace; the founder's museum; the **Observatory** — reclined seats beneath a clear dome, built for nothing but watching the Silver Ceiling.

## The session

No pressure, no fail states, no gating, no saves — every visit is The Day. The park runs on a **schedule** announced by chimes and mechanical flip-boards: Bubble Fountain show every ~12 min, whale pass every ~20, manta flyover, carousel waltz on the hour. The schedule makes the park feel inhabited without ever assigning an objective. Your paper punch-ticket gets stamped per ride — cardstock is the only progress tracking that exists.

## The complete verb list

Walk and look; ride (Bell, Wheel, Torrent, Carousel, Grotto boat, Pearl Line); press/pull/turn the physical levers, valves, cranks; hold things (ticket, pocket park model, ice cream, turtle-food cone, prizes); feed the turtles; play the three midway games; press and collect the eight pennies in a velvet book; toss a coin in the wishing well; sit anywhere. That's the whole game, on purpose.

## Look, sound, feel — six pillars

1. **Sunbeams are architecture.** God rays, caustic glints crawling over marble, the Silver Ceiling overhead. The sun is the art director.
2. **Glass and brass.** Refraction and reflection everywhere; the park is a jewel seen from inside.
3. **Blue is distance.** The aquatic fog gradient makes every vista read as depth; the drop-off gives infinity.
4. **Life is the crowd.** Rays, turtles, jellies, seahorses, and the whale are the guests; motion everywhere — nothing is ever perfectly still.
5. **Two acoustics.** Dreamy, muffled distance versus warm nearby clarity; crossing the waterline audibly swaps worlds. A carousel waltz heard across the lagoon is the signature sound.
6. **The Grotto is the night.** With an eternal afternoon outside, darkness lives underground — bioluminescence gets its stage there.

## Ten postcard moments (the visual contract)

1. The Descent — sky → waterline → the park glowing below, one unbroken shot.
2. The Esplanade flyover — the manta crossing above the glass vault.
3. The Wheel's crest breaking into sunlight, water sheeting off the gondola glass.
4. The Torrent's dive off the shelf edge into open blue nothing.
5. A manta gliding over the Esplanade, its shadow crossing the marble.
6. The carousel waltz heard across the lagoon, lights rippling on the water… of the wishing well.
7. Snell's window from the Observatory — the whole sky compressed into a circle overhead.
8. The whale at the Overlook — shadow first, then the eye.
9. The pearl treasury's galaxy-glow in the Grotto.
10. The Bubble Fountain grand show — columns of light-threaded air over the Tidal Court.

## What this game is NOT

No combat, no death, no timers, no HUD, no quests, no minimap, no characters, no swimming, no day/night cycle, no photo mode, no saves, no horror turn, no survival, no crafting, no economy. One place, one golden afternoon, made as beautiful as we can make it.
